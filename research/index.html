About Me

Currently a Research Assistant at School of Computer Science and Statistics of Trinity College Dublin, and part of the ADAPT Research Center.

I recently finished my Ph.D. in April 2020 and am expected to join as a Postdoctoral Researcher in ADAPT soon. My current research projects and activities primarily include: assisting with the PROTECT ITN project, continuing my PhD work regarding GDPR compliance using semantic web, chairing and contributing to the DPVCG, authoring an update to consent receipt specification. More information about is available in the research projects section. I also maintain a research blog for general articles and discussions.

Projects

this section is work-in-progress
DPVCG
The W3C Data Privacy Vocabularies and Controls Community Group (DPVCG) works towards developing vocabularies arising from privacy laws such as the GDPR. My activities in this include chairing the group and contributing in its deliverable, which currently includes the Data Privacy Vocabulary.
Consent Receipt
The Consent Receipt Specification provides a standardised schema to maintain and document (given) consent, and is maintained by the non-profit Kantara Initiative. My activities in this involve exploring how the consent receipt can be updated with newer privacy laws such as the GDPR and CCPA towards the next iteration of the specification.
GDPR Compliance
This project looks into ways for using Semantic Web technologies for GDPR compliance. My PhD research was structured around this topic, and I will be continuing to work on existing resources as well as researching new applications.
Privacy Policies
This project looks into approaches involving privacy policies - whether for making them easier to understand, annotating them with metadata, repurposing their use for other applications, and an analysis of their contents.
